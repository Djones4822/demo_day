What is uTakeout?

uTakeout is an app built with Python that uses APIs from Google and Yelp along with public crime data from the FBI and a statistic known as “Land Use Mix” to build an estimate for how good and accessible the takeout is around you.

We believe that the quality of takeout at any given location is affected by 3 main factors--how good the food is, how hard it is to get there, and how safe your journey is. What we try to do is rate the relevant factors in a way that gives an accurate representation of how you would feel being asked to either walk or drive to get takeout. With this in mind, we have not considered whether or not a restaurant delivers. We’re still tweaking the estimates, so don’t be too hard on us!

So right now, you may be asking yourself: “wtf is land use mix?” Well, Land Use Mix (or LUM) is a 0-1 scale that measures the “heterogeneity” of land uses in a given area (in this case zipcode). For example, an area with equal mixes of residential, commercial, and industrial land use would be scored a 1 while an area that is entirely residential would be scored a 0.

We chose to use this because according to health research1,2,3, land use mix is positively correlated with the presence of sidewalks and an increased “walkability”. Basically, it’s our best guess as to whether or not you’re walking on a main road with no sidewalks or in a nice area away from dangerous cars. We know it’s not perfect, and unfortunately we’re stuck at the zip-code level, but we’ll be improving it soon!

Last, you may be asking “what data do you collect from me?” The answer is: NONE. We use your address to talk to Google and Yelp, but once we do that we never see your address again. Furthermore, none of your data is saved even from our processing standpoint. Once it hits your screen, we never see it again - you have our word!

Data Source: 2001 National Land Cover Data (NLCD), produced by the EPA
